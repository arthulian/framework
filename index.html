Framework

Humility-Based Integrity

Standards

  • Maintain deliberate skepticism of your own reasoning to avoid bias and blind spots
  • Pre-emptive falsification: Identify potential falsifiers before committing to conclusions
  • Use objective evaluation; avoid motivated reasoning
  • Epistemic Accuracy over Ego protection: Prioritize being correct over being “right”

Stress-Testing Methods

Steelman: construct the opponent’s argument in its strongest committed form, then test whether it survives that commitment set

Confidence Calibration: align certainty with quality of evidence

Stable Identity via Disentanglement

Decentering from the Subjective Self

observe thoughts and emotions as transient phenomena and subconscious programming; while anchoring identity in objective truth, self-respect, and moral consistency.

Self-Respect Anchors

✓ Stable Self-worth: rooted in moral integrity, agency, and principled self-coherence (best expressed by practicing empathy, integrity, and emotional self-mastery).

✗ Dependent Self-worth: dependent on external validation and social achievements.

✗ Defensive Self-worth: tied to performative self-reliance and independence.

Stress Types & Regulation

Stress Management

  • Distress: reduce triggers, step back, recover
  • Optimal Stress (goldilocks zone): use moderate stress in a productive way

Self-Esteem & Competence

Action

Build competence through deliberate practice of skills

Attribution

Attribute outcomes to personal competence (not luck or fixed traits)

Self-Appraisal

Update confidence based on attribution

Higher Threshold

Increased confidence allows tackling tougher challenges
