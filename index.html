2.0

Epistemology: The Truth-Processing Layer

2.1

Uniform criteria: Apply consistent empirical and rational standards to all conclusions.

2.2

Assess propositions based on their likelihood and the robustness of evidence.

2.3

Recognize and validate a continuous chain of reasoning (claims, evidence, and conclusions).

2.4

Evaluate the soundness of arguments and the relevance of evidence.

2.5

Recognize that oversimplifications and binary thinking arise from framing narratives and cognitive distortions, and counter them by applying a nuanced evaluation.

2.6

Epistemological humility: Absence of evidence does not equal proof of absence (unless there are induction-based premises).

3.0

Ethics & Values: The Calibration Layer

3.1

Process factual guilt to correct errors, but explicitly reject shame derived from external blame.

3.2

Fair judgement: use perspective-taking to balance intent against consequence.

4.0

Agency & Attribution: The Performance Layer

4.1

Focus on controllables: shift attribution to skill, effort, and strategy, rather than fixed character.

4.2

Build confidence through mastery: Prioritize evidence of your competence over "identity".

4.3

Decoupling protocol: Base your persistence purely on ongoing outcomes, not on a referendum of your personal value.

5.0

Psychological Flexibility: The Regulation Layer

5.1

Cognitive defusion: Treat internal experiences (thoughts/emotions) as data points, not commands.

5.2

Gradually confront situations and unknowns you typically avoid to reduce fear.

5.3

Expectancy Violation: When anticipated negative outcomes fail to occur, the brain's predictive models are forced to update.
