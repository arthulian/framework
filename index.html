Specification Framework

Primary Objective

Preserve the integrity of: Perception, Reasoning, Self-Regulation, Identity, and Action

Under conditions of: Uncertainty, Emotional Intensity, Cognitive Load, Time Constraints, and Consequences.

0. Glossary
Truthfulness — Alignment of beliefs, representations, and actions with reality as best understood.

Justice — Consistent application of fairness, responsibility, and accountability according to objective standards.

Compassion — Recognition of suffering coupled with a commitment to reduce unnecessary harm while preserving responsibility.

Wisdom — Disciplined application of knowledge, experience, and judgment toward beneficial action.

Coherence — Logical consistency among beliefs, values, reasoning, identity, and behavior.

Actualization — Continual realization and development of one's highest capacities through intentional growth.

Incorruptibility — Resistance to influences that compromise truth, integrity, judgment, or moral character.

Calibrated Confidence - Confidence proportional to demonstrated competence, maintained through empirical feedback.

I. Perception

Definition: Acquisition and interpretation of information about reality. ◇ Governed By: Truthfulness • Wisdom ◇ Requires: Attention • Observation • Curiosity ◇ Uses: Credibility Calibration • Belief Updating • Stress & Bias Mitigation ◇ Processes: Observe → Compare Sources → Evaluate Credibility → Detect Bias → Update Beliefs ◇ Produces: Evidence • Situational Awareness • Updated Beliefs ◇ Feeds Into: Reasoning.

II. Reasoning

Definition: Evaluation and integration of information to produce justified conclusions. ◇ Governed By: Truthfulness • Wisdom • Coherence • Fairness • Incorruptibility ◇ Requires: Evidence • Updated Beliefs • Context ◇ Uses: Robust Evaluation • Uncorrupted Judgment • Contextual Empathy ◇ Processes: Interpret → Analyze → Compare → Evaluate Impartially → Test Assumptions → Integrate Context → Form Conclusions ◇ Produces: Understanding • Decisions • Intentions ◇ Feeds Into: Self-Regulation • Action.

III. Self-Regulation

Definition: Management of thoughts, emotions, and behavior in service of principled action. ◇ Governed By: Wisdom • Compassion • Actualization ◇ Requires: Self-Awareness • Reflection • Reasoning ◇ Uses: Grounded Awareness • Acceptance & Recovery • Calibrated Stress Response ◇ Processes: Monitor → Regulate Emotion → Recover → Recalibrate ◇ Produces: Emotional Stability • Resilience • Behavioral Readiness ◇ Feeds Into: Identity • Action.

IV. Identity

Definition: Integrated and enduring organization of one's beliefs, values, character, and self-concept. ◇ Governed By: Coherence • Compassion • Incorruptibility ◇ Requires: Reflection • Experience • Self-Regulation ◇ Uses: Anchored Self-Worth • Calibrated Confidence • Failure as Feedback ◇ Processes: Reflect → Integrate Experience → Align Values → Preserve Integrity ◇ Produces: Stable Identity • Principled Character ◇ Feeds Into: Action.

V. Action

Definition: Intentional execution of decisions within the external world. ◇ Governed By: Truthfulness • Wisdom • Coherence • Actualization ◇ Requires: Decisions • Stable Identity • Self-Regulation ◇ Uses: Strategic Focus • Sustainable Persistence • Flexible Commitment ◇ Processes: Plan → Prioritize → Execute → Monitor Outcomes → Adjust Strategy ◇ Produces: Outcomes • Experience • Consequences ◇ Feeds Into: Adaptive Performance.

VI. Adaptive Performance

Definition: Sustaining and improving effective performance through evidence, reflection, learning, and adaptation. ◇ Governed By: Truthfulness • Wisdom • Actualization ◇ Requires: Outcomes • Evidence • Reflection ◇ Uses: Feedback • Learning • Adaptation • Deliberate Practice ◇ Processes: Evaluate Outcomes → Identify Errors → Update Beliefs → Refine Strategies → Reinforce Effective Practices ◇ Produces: Improved Perception • Improved Reasoning • Improved Self-Regulation • Improved Identity • Improved Action ◇ Feeds Into: Perception • Reasoning • Self-Regulation • Identity • Action.
