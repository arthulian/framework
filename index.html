Framework

Epistemic Humility

  • Discernment: Identify potential falsifiers before committing to any final conclusions
  • Justice: Contextually weigh the input-outcome ratio between a person's moral intent and the consequences of his actions.
  • Objectivity: Prioritize correctness over proving yourself right, by applying consistent standards.
  • Applying the Golden Rule: Construct the opponent’s argument in its strongest committed form (steelman), then test whether it survives its own set of commitments

Decentering the Ego from Subjectivity

Observer Mindset: View thoughts and emotions as transient phenomena.

Dignity Anchors

✓ Innate Self-worth: Rooted in moral character (expressed as moral integrity, volition, and empathy).

✗ Dependent Self-worth: Dependent on the external validation of social recognition.

✗ Defensive Self-worth: Based on performative self-reliance and defensive self-sufficiency.

Stress Types & Responses

  • Distress: Under overwhelming pressure; step back to reduce triggers and recover.
  • The Optimal zone: Under moderate pressure; stay focused and productive, without becoming overwhelmed.

The Self-Efficacy Loop

1.

Build skills through a consistent handling of challenges.

2.

Credit successes to the demonstrated competence during challenges.

3.

Update self-confidence based on the credited success.

4.

Increased confidence allows tackling tougher challenges
