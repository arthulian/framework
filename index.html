Framework

Section 1
Intellectual & Epistemic Humility
A. Core Principles
Epistemic Pre-commitment
  • Define falsifiers before forming conclusions.
  • Avoid changing the standards that let you move the goalposts.
  • Focus on 'getting it right' rather than 'being right'.
Awareness of Knowledge Limits
  • Recognize what you don't know and challenge assumptions with evidence.
Testing Argument Boundaries
  • Pinpoint weak spots and ensure your reasoning holds under scrutiny.
B. Stress-Testing Methods
Mirror Test
  • Challenge your own views by fairly and accurately presenting the opposing arguments, to foster internal clarity.
Graded Confidence
  • Adjust how strongly you hold beliefs based on the strength of evidence.
Section 2
Equanimity Through Self-Control
A. Observer Mindset
Internal State Awareness
  • Notice your internal states without getting caught up in them, creating a distance between yourself and your emotions.
B. Effort Allocation
Controllable
  • Invest full cognitive resources when actions affect outcomes.
Partial Control
  • Scale effort according to realistic level of influence.
Uncontrollable
  • Refrain from effort to prevent wasted energy and instability.
C. Internal Validation
Character-Based Self-Esteem
  • Anchor your self-worth in your efforts, values, and authenticity, rather than external validation or results.
Section 3
Resilience Through Stress Inoculation
A. Stress Response Profiles
Distress
  • Defend the integrity of your system under pressure or threat.
Eustress
  • Use tests and challenges, as tools to improve your structure.
B. Hormesis
The Goldilocks Zone
  • Stimulating challenges at moderate intensity with periods of recovery encourages personal growth.
C. Self-Efficacy Cycle
Confidence Building
  • Confidence grows through repeated successful adaptation to stress.
Caution
Abandoning agency undermines the ability to adapt and may lead to learned helplessness. When in doubt, Section 3 should override the "Refrain from Effort" rule in Section 2.
