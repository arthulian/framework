Definitions

1.0
Epistemic-Processing
▼
Principles
  • Logical Coherence: Follow an unbroken logical chain in which every assertion stems directly from supporting facts or prior premises.
  • Empirical Standards: Claims are based solely on verifiable observations and rational methodologies.
  • Probabilistic Assessment: Claims are assessed using probabilistic degrees of confidence rather than definitive binary (true/false) categories.
2.0
Ethical Calibration
▼
Principles
  • Responsibility: Evaluate the potential impacts of your actions and inactions by weighing short-term objectives against long-term consequences.
  • Perspective-Taking: Objectively analyze allocentric viewpoints to avoid bias in judgment, ensuring fairness towards all parties.
  • Value Alignment: Integrate personal ethics into daily choices, making sure decisions resonate with core values.
  • Intellectual Humility: a lack of proof for a claim does not automatically disprove it, unless there is sufficient reason.
3.0
Effectiveness
▼
Principles
  • Attribution: Attribute outcomes to effort, skills, and strategy rather than uncontrollable factors.
  • Evidence-Based Confidence: Base confidence levels primarily on tangible achievements and proven capabilities.
  • Decoupling: Maintain detachment from outcome-based evaluations, interpreting setbacks as feedback rather than a reflection of intrinsic self-worth.
4.0
Self-Regulation
▼
Principles
  • Cognitive Flexibility: Adopt an observer mindset toward intrusive thoughts, distinguishing them clearly from binding directives.
  • Incremental Challenge: Gradually expose yourself to novel challenges to build tolerance and reduce anxiety surrounding uncertain scenarios.
  • Falsifiability: Regularly test hypotheses by seeking counter-evidence and avoiding false dichotomies.
  • Adaptive Learning: Revise beliefs if real-world results diverge significantly from preconceived expectations.
  • Sunk-Cost Avoidance: Evaluate every situation assuming baseline conditions are contingent, not a guarantee.
