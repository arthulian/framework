Self-Regulation

0. SELF-REGULATION

Sustains goal-directed behavior by (a) inhibiting impulsive responses, (b) directing and sustaining attention, and (c) regulating emotion under pressure, distraction, or uncertainty.

1. Sense-Making

Constructs a coherent working model of reality by (a) integrating metacognitive signals (confidence, bias cues, emotional arousal), (b) incorporating allocentric perspectives (other parties' models, base rates), (c) reconciling conflicts across inputs, and (d) revising preconceptions when evidence demands it.

1a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates assertions by (a) estimating the prior plausibility of its proposition, (b) assessing source reliability, (c) separating claim, evidence, and inference as distinct justificatory layers, and (d) evaluating logical validity and evidential relevance.

Behavioral markers: (a) distinguishes claim from evidence, (b) seeks base rates, (c) attempts falsification, (d) tracks uncertainty explicitly.

1b. Objectivity

Identifies uneven evidentiary standards in belief formation, then applies (a) empirical criteria (measurement, replication, base rates) and (b) rational criteria (veil of ignorance, non-contradiction, Bayes-consistent updating) uniformly to favored and disfavored conclusions, regardless of personal preference or prior commitment.

1c. Fairness

Maintains impartial balance in reciprocal interactions by weighing (a) empathy (context, constraints, intent) against (b) accountability norms (reciprocity, consequences, repair), irrespective of status, convenience, mood, or sense of moral position.

Example: Admits own mistakes to prevent double standards and hypocrisy.

1d. Self-Reflection

Analyzes past outcomes through accurate causal attribution, distinguishing personal factors (choices, efforts, behaviors) from external circumstances, emphasizing process and controllable elements in order to (a) detect recurring patterns, (b) identify true levers of influence, and (c) translate insights into concrete, testable action rules; while avoiding rumination by prioritizing immediate, actionable implementation.

1e. Self-Efficacy

Maintains confidence aligned with evidence by (a) grounding self-belief firmly in verified past competencies and achievements, (b) framing setbacks as skill gaps rather than as personal flaws, and (c) peridically re-testing abilities to confirm current capability and keep skills sharp.

Examples: (a) integrates achievements into self-concept, (b) treats setbacks as training data.

2. Self-Discipline

Applies self-regulation consistently to align behavior with long-term goals and principles by (a) setting clear priorities, (b) sustaining execution, and (c) guarding against both rigidity and complacency.

2a. Temperance

Manages willpower as a finite resource by (a) allocating effort according to current strategic importance rather than sunk costs, (b) minimizing unnecessary decision load and temptation exposure, and (c) choosing regulation strategies that preserve capacity for highest-priority objectives.

Example: Compresses emotions into a single label to restore cognitive focus.

2b. Perseverance

Sustains effort amid adversity by (a) overriding avoidance impulses, (b) reaffirming commitment to a valued outcome, and (c) using deliberate exposure and skill-development to reduce fear-driven withdrawal over time.

Examples: (a) treats fear as trainable physiological signals, (b) employs graduated exposure to build lasting resilience.
