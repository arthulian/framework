FRAMEWORK

100%
1. Intellectual & Epistemic Humility
A. Core Principles
  • •Epistemic Pre-commitment:
    • ›Define falsifiers before forming conclusions.
    • ›Remove loopholes that allow you to move the goalposts.
    • ›Prioritize 'getting it right' over 'being right'.
  • •Recognizing Limits:
    • ›Acknowledge any gaps in knowledge.
    • ›Identify the boundary conditions where your argument ceases to apply.
    • ›Notice when your justification method contradicts itself.
B. Stress-Testing Methods
  • •Mirror Test:Steelman opposing views with high fidelity to stress-test your own logic.
  • •Graded Confidence:Let the quality of evidence guide how strongly you hold a belief.
2. Self-Control & Equanimity
A. Observer Mindset
  • • Notice internal states without fusing with them; separate impulses from commands.
B. Effort Allocation
  • •Controllable: Invest full cognitive resources when actions reliably influence outcomes.
  • ›Partial Control: Scale effort according to realistic level of influence.
  • ›Uncontrollable: Refrain from effort to prevent wasted energy and instability.
C. Identity Setting
  • • Ground self-image in your volition (intentional actions) rather than external outcomes.
3. Stress Inoculation & Resilience
A. Stress Response Profiles
    • ›Threat: Defend the integrity of your framework under pressure.
    • ›Tests: Utilize contradictions and feedback as tools to sharpen the framework.
    • ›Varying Challenges: Apply learned strategies across new contexts.
B. Optimal Challenge
  • •Moderate, diverse stressors create the strongest adaptation curve.
C. Self-Efficacy Cycle
  • •Confidence grows through repeated successful adaptation to stress.
Caution: Abandoning agency undermines the ability to adapt and may lead to learned helplessness.
