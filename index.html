Framework

I. EVALUATE EVIDENCE

  • Critical Thinking: Categorize the plausibility of assertions as either factual or manufactured; Check the source credibility; Demarcate between evidence, inference, and claim; Match confidence to evidential support.
  • Sense-Making: Reconcile discrepancies between raw sensory input and a-priori intuition, allowing common sense to take shape through coherent schemas.

II. TEST ASSUMPTIONS

  • Discernment: Anticipates potential falsifiers before jumping to any premature conclusions.
  • Moral Justice: Contextually weighs the input-outcome ratio of a person’s moral intent and the consequences of his actions.
  • Objectivity: Let correctness be the measure of proving yourself right. Identify uneven evidentiary standards in belief formation. Apply empirical and rational criteria uniformly to judge information impartially.
  • Golden Rule of Argumentation: Reconstruct the opposing argument in its most coherent, committed form (steelman). Then test whether it survives its own commitment set.
  • Veil of ignorance: Reduce bias by deliberately hiding specific, identifying details about the people involved, so that choices are evaluated purely on their objective merits.

III. SEPARATE THE SELF FROM EGO

  • Observer Mindset: Maintain a third-person perspective during interaction, reframing mental states as transient phenomena, while allowing for identification with core values.

IV. ALIGN VALUES WITH BEHAVIOR

  • Intrinsic Self-Worth (the ideal): Define worth by consistency with self-chosen standards of integrity, not external validation or justification.
  • Defensive Self-Worth: Cease all performative self-sufficiency and defensive self-reliance intended to convince others; redirect energy from justification to intrinsic activity.
  • Moral Integrity: Committed regulation of behavior that aligns actions with core values.

V. OPTIMIZE STRESS

  • Distress: Under overwhelming pressure, disengage from escalating stimuli through boundary setting.
  • The Optimal Zone: Under moderate pressure, introduce controlled, low-stakes challenges to reinforce low-fragility and resilience.

VI. UPDATE CONFIDENCE

  • The Structure: Self-confidence calibrated by feedback that accurately connects outcomes to efforts.
  • The Loop: Build skills by overcoming challenges. Attribute achieved outcomes to your own vested efforts. Update your personal capability baseline (confidence) based on these verifiable results. Improved confidence enables tackling tougher challenges.
  • Loop Guardrail: Setbacks define skill deficits, not personal deficits.

VII. ACT WITH PERSISTENCE

  • Internal Locus of Control: Treat mental energy as a limited resource by directing effort toward events that can be influenced rather than dwelling on sunk costs.
  • Perseverance: A steady persistence of action toward a specific goal, despite difficulties. It confronts fear-driven avoidance through a gradual exposure to feared stimuli, which increases resilience.
