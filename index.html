1.0 Epistemic-Processing
  • •Logical Coherence:Follow an unbroken logical chain in which every assertion stems directly from supporting facts or prior premises.
  • •Empirical Grounding:Base claims on observable data, reproducible methods, and measurable outcomes.
  • •Graduated Confidence:Evaluate claims via degrees of confidence, applying logical and evidentiary standards.
2.0 Ethical Calibration
  • •Self-accountability:Evaluate the fairness of your conduct by weighing immediate volitions against long-term consequences.
  • •Perspective-Taking:Objectively analyze allocentric viewpoints to maintain impartiality during all interactions.
  • •Value Consistency:Integrate personal ethics into daily choices, making sure decisions resonate with core values.
3.0 Effectiveness
  • •Locus of Control:Attribute outcomes to effort, skills, and strategy rather than uncontrollable factors.
  • •Evidence-Based Confidence:Base confidence levels in proportion to demonstrated competence.
  • •Decoupling:Objectively interpret setbacks as feedback rather than as a reflection of intrinsic self-worth.
4.0 Self-Regulation
  • •Impulse Control:Adopt an observer mindset toward mental states, distinguishing them clearly from binding directives.
  • •Hormesis:Gradually expose yourself to novel challenges to build tolerance and reduce anxiety.
  • •Falsifiability:Test hypotheses by seeking counterevidence while avoiding false dichotomies.
  • •Baseline Certainty:Evaluate every situation assuming baselines depend on contingent conditions.
