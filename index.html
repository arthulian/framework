INTELLECTUAL HUMILITY
Core Principles
  • •Empirical Grounding:Claims must be weighted by strength of evidence and be subject to falsifiable predictions.
  • •Dynamic Confidence:Confidence is measured in proportion to validated evidence.
  • •Transparency:Uncertainty, knowledge gaps, and boundary conditions must always be disclosed.
Stress-Testing Methods
  • •Counterevidence:Systematically search for opposing data; but excessive searching can lead to analysis paralysis.
  • •Graded Hierarchy:Weight conclusions proportionally to the quality of the evidence.
EQUANIMITY
Control Mechanisms
  • •Observer-like Distancing:Monitor thoughts and emotions non-reactively.
  • •Effort Allocation:
    • ›Controllables: Allocate effort fully
    • ›Partial Control: Scale effort proportionally
    • ›Uncontrollables: Redirect effort away from intended control
  • •Decoupling:Anchor self-esteem in intentions, not in uncontrollable outcomes.
Principle: Focusing effort on uncontrollable variables is considered inefficient and destabilizing.
RESILIENCE
Stress Inoculation
  • •Stressor -> Response
    • ›Threat -> Adaptive
    • ›Adversity -> Conditionally adaptive
    • ›Unpredictability -> Non-adaptive
  • •Optimal Conditions:Moderate, predictable stress with adequate recovery.
  • •Self-Efficacy:A trainable capability that develops through a cycle of action, outcome, and adjusted perception.
Warning: Renouncing agency converts unpredictability into learned helplessness.
