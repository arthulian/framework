1.0 Epistemic-Processing
  • •Logical Chain:Assertions must derive directly from supporting facts.
  • •Empirical Grounding:Ground claims in measurable, reproducible dat.
  • •Graduated Confidence:Evaluate claims via degrees of confidence that match the strength or reliability of evidence.
2.0 Ethical Calibration
  • •Self-accountability:Weigh immediate intentions against long-term consequences.
  • •Perspective-Taking:analyze allocentric viewpoint to maintain impartiality.
  • •Value Consistency:Choices should uphold core values as preserved constants. Decisions must reflect fundamental principles.
3.0 Effectiveness
  • •Locus of Control:Focus your energy on your effort/work, not on what you cannot control.
  • •Evidence-Based Confidence:Proportion your confidence to your demonstrated competence.
  • •Decoupling:Separate your sense of self-worth from setbacks and opinions
4.0 Self-Regulation
  • •Observer Mindset:Cultivate the ability to observe your thoughts and emotions without immediate judgement.
  • •Hormesis:Controlled exposure to challenges enhance resilience and adaptability.
  • •Falsifiability:Actively seek counterevidence to test your own hypothesis.
