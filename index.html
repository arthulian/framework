BLUEPRINT

I. EVALUATE EVIDENCE

  • Critical Thinking: Determine the plausibility of assertions; Check for source credibility; Distinguish between evidence, inference, and claim; Match confidence to evidential support.
  • Sense-Making: Use mental constructs to cement a state of comprehension and resolve the cognitive friction between sensory input and intuition.

II. TEST ASSUMPTIONS

  • Falsification: Identify potential counter-arguments before forming conclusions.
  • Situational Justice: Weigh the input-outcome ratio of the person’s moral intent with the consequences of his actions, through contextual empathy.
  • Objectivity: Focus on factual accuracy; Avoid self-justification or motivated rationalization; Avoid adopting double standards of verification during belief formation by applying empirical and criteria uniformly.
  • The Principle of Charity: Steelman the opponent’s argument in its most committed form, in order to test whether it survives against its own commitment set.

III. SELF-COMPASSION

  • Observer Mindset: Cultivate a mindful, suspended judgement of the present moment, reframing thoughts and emotions as transient mental phenomena, while still allowing identification with archetypal values.
  • Common Humanity: Situate your struggles within the shared human condition. Imperfection and vulnerability are universal characteristics of human existence rather than isolating flaws.
  • Active Self-Kindness: De-escalate internal pressure through realistic understanding of your current limitations, intentionally pausing to recover and restore your baseline functioning.

IV. FOUNDATIONS OF SELF-WORTH

  • Stable Self-Reference: Self-worth is derived from the volition of character to embody the transcendentals (truth, justice, love, wisdom, holiness, complete & undivided being, proportion [symmetry, scale, resonance], actualized potential, immutable fidelity, wisdom, and holiness), which are the source and standards of all true worth.
  • Defensive Self-Worth (failure mode): Avoid defensive self-reliance or performative self-sufficiency, which seek external validation.

V. OPTIMIZE STRESS

  • Distress: Under overwhelming pressure, disengage from adverse stimuli through boundary setting.
  • The Optimal Zone: Under moderate pressure, introduce controlled, low-stakes challenges to reinforce resilience (low-fragility).

VI. UPDATE CONFIDENCE

  • Structure: Self-confidence calibrated by feedback that accurately links outcomes to efforts.
  • The loop: 1. Build skills by overcoming challenges.
    2. Attribute achieved objectives to my own allotted efforts.
    3. Update my capability baseline (my confidence) based on those empirical results.
    4. Improved confidence enables tackling tougher challenges.
  • Loop guardrail: Interpret setbacks as skill deficits, not personal deficits.

VII. ACT WITH PERSISTENCE

  • Conserve Mental Energy: Allot effort to influenceable events, rather than dwelling on sunk costs. The goal is not to control my reality, but to respond intelligently within my circumstances.
  • Perseverance: Steady persistence in a course of action, despite difficulties. It overcomes fear-driven avoidance through a gradual exposure to feared stimuli.
