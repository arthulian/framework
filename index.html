Rational Intuition

1.0
Truth-Processing
Ensure logical rigor and empirical grounding in reasoning.
▼
Propositions
  • Logical Coherence: Each claim should flow from evidence to conclusion, avoiding logical gaps.
  • Empirical Standards: Use data and rational criteria to evaluate claims.
  • Likelihood Over Binary Truth: Assess claims probabilistically, not as absolute truths.
  • Epistemological Humility: Absence of evidence does not equal evidence of absence.
2.0
Calibration
Align actions with ethical standards and accountability.
▼
Propositions
  • Responsibility: For each action, evaluate intent, assess the resulting consequence, and fulfill the ensuing duty.
  • Impartial Judgement: Use perspective-taking to balance intentions and consequences.
  • Integrity: Define core values and ensure they align with goals and actions.
3.0
Performance
Optimize performance by focusing on controllable factors.
▼
Propositions
  • Attribution: Prioritize effort, skills, and strategy over uncontrollable factors.
  • Skill-Based Confidence: Trust demonstrated ability, not self-assessment.
  • Outcome Decoupling: Separate self-worth from results; treat negative outcomes as feedback.
4.0
Regulation
Manage cognitive and emotional responses for adaptive behavior.
▼
Propositions
  • Cognitive Distancing: Treat thoughts as data, not commands.
  • Gradual Exposure: Confront uncertainties incrementally to reduce fear.
  • Falsifiability: Claims must be testable; avoid overgeneralizations.
  • Expectancy Violation: Update your own mental models when reality contradicts expectations.
