Specification Framework

Primary Objective: To preserve the integrity of perception, reasoning, self-regulation, motivation, and action — under any condition that increases uncertainty, emotional[...]

I. Perceptual Integrity

Goal: Evaluate evidence to preserve accurate perception.
  • ├── Judge claims by identifying recurring patterns.
  • ├── Reassess credibility when predictions consistently fail.
  • ├── Distinguish observation from inference and unsupported claims.
  • ├── Use counterfactual reasoning to examine apparent conflicts between evidence and emotional reactions.
  • ├── Resist attentional narrowing produced by stress.
  • ├── Prioritize predictive accuracy over narrative coherence.
  • └── Update beliefs proportionally to evidence.

II. Cognitive Integrity

Goal: Analyze asumotions to preserve sound judgment.
  • ├── Consider the strongest opposing arguments before reaching conclusions.
  • ├── Evaluate both intent and consequences with contextual empathy.
  • ├── Prioritize factual accuracy.
  • ├── Avoid self-justification and motivated reasoning.
  • ├── Apply consistent standards of verification.
  • ├── Evaluate whether even the strongest opposing arguments remain internally consistent.
  • ├── Separate truth-seeking from identity protection.
  • └── Reject conclusions that cannot withstand steelmanning.

III. Emotional Regulation

Self-Compassion

Goal: Preserve executive functioning during emotional activation.
  • ├── Practice an observer's perspective of the present moment.
  • ├── Reframe thoughts and emotions as transient mental states.
  • ├── Allow deliberate action rather than automatic reaction.
  • ├── Remember that imperfection and vulnerability are universal.
  • ├── Accept current limitations without unnecessary self-pressure.
  • ├── Treat recovery as part of sustained engagement, not avoidance.
  • └── Reduce emotional interference without suppressing emotion.

Stress Regulation

Goal: Match response to current capacity.
  • ├── Under high stress, disengage through healthy boundaries.
  • ├── Under moderate stress, use controlled exposure to build resilience and assess capacity.
  • ├── Use recovery to restore future performance rather than escape difficulty.
  • └── Increase exposure gradually as resilience develops.

IV. Stable Identity

Anchored Self-Worth

Goal: Preserve stable self-worth independent of outcomes.
  • ├── Anchor self-worth in the deliberate development of character.
  • ├── Understand character as embodying the eight transcendentals: Truth, Justice, Love, Wisdom, Wholeness, Mathematical Order, Actualized Poten[...]
  • ├── Treat the eight transcendentals as both the source and standard of genuine worth.
  • ├── Avoid grounding self-worth in external validation, including defensive self-reliance and performative self-sufficiency.
  • └── Keep identity independent of success, failure, praise, criticism, competence, or outcomes.

Confidence Calibration

Goal: Maintain accurate expectations of present capability.
  • ├── Calibrate confidence using empirical feedback.
  • ├── Connect effort with outcomes.
  • ├── Build skills through challenge.
  • ├── Attribute success to effort supported by evidence.
  • ├── Recognize demonstrated competence to reduce impostor syndrome.
  • ├── Use setbacks to identify skill deficits rather than deficiencies in self-worth.
  • ├── Let increased confidence support progressively harder challenges.
  • └── Maintain confidence as an estimate of demonstrated competence rather than intrinsic worth.

V. Adaptive Action

Goal: Sustain effective action under changing conditions.
  • ├── Focus effort on influenceable events.
  • ├── Avoid dwelling on sunk costs.
  • ├── Aim for intelligent response rather than complete control.
  • ├── Build perseverance through gradual exposure.
  • ├── Regulate persistence with self-kindness to prevent burnout.
  • ├── Preserve flexibility without abandoning commitment.
  • └── Adapt strategy before abandoning purpose.

VI. Key Relations

  • ├── Perceptual Integrity
    • ├── Recurring patterns → credibility assessment; failed predictions → credibility reassessment.
    • └── Counterfactual reasoning → reconciles evidence with emotional reactions.
  • ├── Cognitive Integrity
    • ├── Accurate perception → reliable judgment → effective decisions.
    • ├── Strong counterarguments precede sound conclusions; steelmanning → stronger evaluation, and conclusions that can't survive it are[...]
    • ├── Intent and consequences, weighed together → complete moral evaluation.
    • └── Identity protection → weakens judgment — the failure mode this system guards against.
  • ├── Emotional Regulation
    • ├── Observer perspective → reframing thoughts/emotions as transient → deliberate action (not impulsive reaction).
    • ├── Acceptance → recovery → sustained engagement.
    • ├── High stress → healthy boundaries and disengagement; moderate stress → controlled exposure → resilience.
    • └── Recovery → restored future performance capacity.
  • ├── Stable Motivation
    • ├── Character → self-worth, with the transcendentals as both source and standard.
    • ├── Feedback → confidence, via the effort–outcome link.
    • ├── Setbacks → skill deficits, not self-worth deficits.
    • ├── Stable self-worth → performance resilience, independent of outcomes.
    • └── Calibrated confidence → capacity for progressively greater challenge.
  • └── Adaptive Action
    • ├── Gradual exposure → perseverance.
    • ├── Self-kindness → regulated persistence → burnout prevention.
    • └── Adapting strategy, rather than abandoning purpose → sustained performance.

VII. Performance Loop

Goal: Sustain adaptive performance through recalibration.
  • ├── Reality → Evidence → Perception → Reasoning → Decision → Action → Outcome.
  • ├── Feedback updates Confidence & Competence.
  • ├── Greater Challenge drawn from calibrated capability.
  • └── Identity stays stable while capability is continuously updated by feedback.
