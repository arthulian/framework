Definitions

Sense-Making:

Extracts perceptual invariants to form symbolic concepts and organizes those concepts into relational schemas that support interpretation, inference, and evaluation..

Critical Thinking:

Evaluates propositions by assessing their likelihood and evidential warrant, while determining whether arguments are sound and evidence is relevant to the propositions. This process clarifies the relations among claims, evidence, and inference.

Objectivity:

Mitigates motives by identifying uneven evidentiary standards, then uniformly applying empirical and rational criteria to all conclusions through self-critique.

Fairness:

Empathetically weighing the actor's intent against their accountability relative to the consequences, in reciprocal interactions.

Self-Reflection:

Causal attribution that involves separating personal choices from external circumstances, identifying recurring patterns and controllable levers of influence, and converting insights into action.

Self-Efficacy:

Grounds self-belief in past achievements, frames setbacks as temporary skill gaps rather than personal flaws, and periodically reassesses skills to pit subjective belief against objective competence.

Self-Discipline:

The establishment of priorities and the persistent implementation of goals, achieved by voluntarily inhibiting “wants” to pursue “shoulds” amid internal conflict, thus aligning behavior to long-term objectives.

Perseverance:

Goal-directed persistence of action, through the overriding of fear-based avoidance, committing to valued outcomes, and utilizing gradual exposure and skill-development to reduce fear-driven withdrawal.
