This framework describes a progression from Intellectual Humility to Equanimity to Resilience.
PHASE I INTELLECTUAL HUMILITY
Core Principles
  • •Empirical Grounding:Claims must be weighted by evidence strength and be subject to falsifiable predictions.
  • •Dynamic Confidence:Confidence is measured in proportion to validated evidence.
  • •Transparency:Uncertainty, knowledge gaps, and boundary conditions must be explicitly disclosed.
Evaluation Methods
  • •Stress-Testing:Recognize fallibility and challenge claims.
  • •Counterevidence:Deliberately search for opposing data to avoid confirmation bias.
  • •Graded Hierarchy:Weighting conclusions proportionally to the quality of the evidence.
PHASE II EQUANIMITY
Psychological Mechanisms
  • •Observer-like Detachment:Monitor thoughts and emotions non-reactively.
  • •Effort Allocation:Controllables (engage), Partial Control (proportional effort), Uncontrollables (disengage).
  • •Decoupling:Basing self-worth away from uncontrollable outcomes and towards internally anchored intentions.
Key Insight: Focusing effort on uncontrollable variables is identified as destabilizing and inefficient.
PHASE III RESILIENCE
Hormesis & Stress
  • •Optimal Adaptation:The result of moderate, predictable stress with adequate recovery.
  • •Self-Confidence:A trainable capacity built through a feedback loop of action, outcome, and adjusted judgment.
  • •Stress Categorization:Threat (adaptive), Hardship (conditionally adaptive), and Unpredictability (non-adaptive).
