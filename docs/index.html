1. Self-Regulation

The capacity to focus attention, manage impulses, and adjust behavior in response to any internal or external demands.

Examples

  • Delays gratification by practicing restraint, preventing impulsivity
  • Reframes attention or impulses when tempted, distracted, or emotionally reactive

2. Sense-Making

Synthesizes varied data into a single cohesive perception of reality.

Examples

  • Constructs rationales that endure the test of time
  • Searches for truth instead of self-justifying rationalizations

2a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates proof, consistency, probability, consensus, and relevance in competing claims.

  • Considers new perspectives with a willingness to adjust personal beliefs
  • Subordinates motivated reasoning to truth

2b. Objectivity

Identifies susceptibility to bias and applies empirical and rational standards uniformly.

  • Applies the “veil of ignorance”
  • Prevents desire, identity, or attachment from overriding standards

2c. Fairness

Balances empathetic regard with accountable obligation across reciprocal exchanges.

  • Avoids both prejudice and favoritism
  • Admits mistakes to prevent double standards and hypocrisy

2d. Self-Reflection

Turns lived experiences into lasting insights without rumination or paralysis.

  • Uses if/then decisions from past experiences to guide future situations

2e. Self-Efficacy

Confidence grounded in accurate assessment of one’s proficiency.

  • Integrates skills and achievements into one’s sense of self
  • Treats setbacks as lack of skill, not lack of self-worth

3. Self-Discipline

Sustained application of self-regulation to align behavior with long-term goals.

3a. Perseverance

Maintains exertion and resolve amid hardship or challenge.

  • Treats fear response as a skill to refine
  • Confronts fear gradually and recurrently

3b. Temperance

Manages energy as a limited resource without evasion or apathy.

  • Allocates 80% of effort to most valued priorities
  • Summarizes emotions into one word to remain focused
